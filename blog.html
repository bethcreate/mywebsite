BLOG 1 - MARRIAGE

What do you think about Patricia and Abel's marriage?"Born a
crime" by Trevor Noah. As I was going through the book I
found out
that Patricia being a single parent she was an
independent woman.
She took care of her son and her mother and
she never depended on else for provision.
Despite the fact that she was facing some challenges as a
single parent, she does not forget her purpose in life.
She remains focused and this made her a happy mother always.
Patricia meets a friend Abel who was a mechanic and he becomes her
best friend as he used to fix her car every time it used to break
down. Their friendship went on for a long time until when
Patricia decides to get married to Abel. Abel came
from Tzaneen where women were only raised for marriage.
I wonder what convinced Patricia to get married to him because he
was a bhang addict and he was very huge and strong. Trevor says
Abel was very hardworking and through his hard work the mother loved him so much.
Patricia thought if they could combine their efforts together
they could have something better. Patricia's marriage to Abel shocks Trevor
in that he never expected them to get married. After
Trevors' biological father had abandoned them Patricia promised Trevor
never to get married again. Do you think it was right for Patricia
to get married to Abel?

Every choice has consequences. After Abel and Patricia's
union in marriage Abel makes few reforms.
He stops
smoking bhang and starts taking alcohol. This is the moment
when Abel started becoming wild. He became worse
than he used to
be. I remember the time when Trevor fogged the mother's
signature on the school documents. Trevor says the
way Abel punished him had never happened to him before.
He says "Abel never hit people with a punch".
He grabbed Trevor's skin on his arms and punched it between
his thumb and forefinger and twisted hard. This made Trevor
have nightmares for a long period of time and he started getting
scared of Abel. They started living a hide and sick kind of life.
This is when Abel starts bringing in the mentality from his village.
They believe that a man should be in control of everything.
He now starts treating Patricia in an awkward way where
he even goes ahead to hitting her.

I find it difficult to understand what continue making
Patricia stick into this marriage after her first beating from Abel.
Of which, Trevor's dad had never done to her before.
Patricia's business starts going down since Abel had now taken
over everything and he was in control. He beats up Patricia
the second time and this time round Patricia takes herself to
the police station. The police never took any action instead
they told her to go and solve their issue at home.
That was so bad since he had beaten her so badly and she had
bristles all over her face. This continues for so long
conflicts after conflicts until the time when Abel buys a gun finally
shoots Patricia living unconsciously without minding her
whereabouts. The way Abel took over the rule over
Patricia's life was so terrifying.

Abel being brought up in the village where women were being
seen as a stepping stone brings the same
mentality into Patricia's life.
I stand on
the fact that marriage should not be a cage of slavery and sabotage.
As a woman whether your partner believes in tradition
marriage it does't matter. Your values should always stand for you.

BORN A CRIME by Trevor Noah is an interesting book that
I have ever read. It is a book filled with a mixture of
emotions, happy and sad moments, and all this have been used
to at list pass a message to the readers. Trevor has used hunour
in this novel to make it flow with the readers. He uses hunour
to make new friends and also on another side it has helped
him get out of trouble during difficult situations.

Trevor says back in Soweto he was the only white child the rest
were blacks. This helped him make many friends in that
whenever black kids saw him they would yell"idonda yomulungu"
meaning "The White Man".Some of them would run away others would
call their parents to come to look and others would run up and
try to touch him to see if he was real, other kids had genuinely
no clue white a white person was. He also says ninety-nine point
nine percent were black and he was famous in his neighborhood
because of his skin color. He was unique and people would give
directions using him as a landmark .
